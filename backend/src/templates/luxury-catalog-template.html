History of Weaving
in India

India's rug weaving tradition dates back to the Mughal era, around the 16th century, when Persian artistry was elegantly fused with local craftsmanship.

Each region, from the lush valleys of Kashmir to the vibrant deserts of Rajasthan, boasts distinct styles and intricate designs that tell unique stories.

For example, Kashmir is renowned for its silk carpets with Persian-inspired motifs, while Rajasthan's Dhurrie rugs are celebrated for their bold geometric patterns.

Skilled artisans meticulously hand-knot wool and silk, often taking several months to complete a single piece. These exquisite rugs not only adorn homes but also symbolize India's rich history and enduring commitment to the art of weaving.
